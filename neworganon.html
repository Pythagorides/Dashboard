Back in the early ’90s, the great logician Graeme Forbes, with impeccable Balliol irony, had a one-panel comic taped to his office door: Rodin’s The Thinker captioned “Aristotle Trying to Think of Frege.” Naturally, this suggested the straightforward doctoral project of thinking my way back in the other direction—from the principle results of Mathematical Logic to somewhere in the vicinity of Metaphysics Μ & Ν.

Sometime around 1996, my resulting dissertation prospectus was approved with the modest title, A Philosophical and Mathematical Theory of Truth. Alas, in temperament I’ve always been more like the mad Wittgenstein than the stoic Frege, and in techné, more like the foolhardy Xenophon than the careful Plato. So the project took twenty years.

Nevertheless, it has now been completed as promised—with the small exception that, instead of a theory of truth, it became a model. Twenty years ago I loosed that arrow of truth. It is now up to the careful reader to see how close I have come to my mark.

D is a collection of well-formed formulae (wffs). The types of wffs include statements of fact, mathematical formulae, financial transactions, laboratory data entries, and more. These wffs are organized into a set of .pdf documents with an accompanying .xmp metadata library.

The .pdfs are ordered and re-ordered to reveal various properties of the system. There are linear arrangements of D as well as complex hierarchical structures. Subdomains are filtered by relevance to properties such as financials, clinical data, and research data.

The .xmp metadata tracks every occurrence of each .pdf and its component wffs. Every part of the system always knows its place in the ordered whole (τάξις). Each .pdf filename begins with a date prefix indicating when that data entered D, allowing for temporal and semantic comparisons.

For example, the model can show what was being reported in the news while certain conversations were taking place, or reveal that specific financial transactions coincided with clinical events (συμμετρία). The .pdfs in D are also broken down into their component wffs in spreadsheets and data logs, and summarized in various dashboards.

Thus, we have high-level views of the entire model and its arrangement, down to finely granular views of singular statements of fact (ὡρισμένον).

I is the interpretation function of #NewOrganon. It assigns meaning to all words and names in D, and determines the truth values of all statements of fact. For the most part, I aligns with the natural language reading of D.

The exception is that some names in D are pseudonyms. They appear to refer to one person but actually refer to another. Whether a name refers to one person or another is critical to determining truth, deception, perjury, and testimony. These pseudonyms cannot be distinguished prima facie, but are tracked in the metadata.

ἐπεὶ δὲ τὸ ἀγαθὸν καὶ τὸ καλὸν ἕτερον (τὸ μὲν γὰρ ἀεὶ ἐν πράξει, τὸ δὲ καλὸν καὶ ἐν τοῖς ἀκινήτοις） — Aristot. Met. [13.1078α]

The differentiation of hepatocytes and biliary epithelial cells (BEC) from embryonic hepatoblasts is regulated by a sequenced interaction of transcription factors known as “hepatocyte nuclear factors” (HNF). HNF-6, followed by HNF-1b, initiates BEC differentiation. HNF4 activates hepatocyte-specific factors and contributes to hepatocyte maintenance.

These observations suggest a homeostatic relationship between hepatocyte and BEC transcriptional activity. Damage to hepatocytes should upregulate HNF-4 in BEC, driving differentiation toward hepatocytes. Conversely, damage to BEC should upregulate HNF-6 in hepatocytes, driving differentiation in the opposite direction. These shifts should correspond to expression patterns observable during hepatogenesis.

The goal of the #PrometheusProject is to model epithelial differentiation in hepatogenesis and liver regeneration, guiding the rational design of transplantable human livers (ἀεὶ ἐν πράξει).
